Background

Lebron Raymone James is a profesional basketball player for the Los Angeles Lakers. He was born on December 30th 1984. His nickname King James comes from his dominance on the court. He is the all time leading scorer with around 51,000 points showing that nobody can stop him. He is also in the top 25 in rebounds in a career all time with 11,940 boards. He is in the top 5 with assists all time with 11,838 dished out dimes as well as in the top 10 in steals all time with 2,384 balls taken. He has 4 Most Valuable Player awards and he won rookie of the year. He has won 4 NBA Championships and he has won all 4 Championships Most Valueable Players as well.

When Lebron was yound he moved in with his football coach Frank Walker who introduced Lebron to Basketball. Due to all the moving in third grade Lebron missed 80 days alone. In elementary school Lebron and his three friends; Dru Joyce III, Sian Cotton, and Willie Mcgee called themselves the Fab Four and promised to play basketball together in High School. Lebron found his love for sports at age 9 where he played football adn he scores 18 touchdowns in just 6 games and later joined the Northeast Ohio Shooting Stars to play basketball.

By the time Lebron was in Middle School he was known world wide as a great athlete and when he was just 16 he was raked as the best 16 year old basketball player in the counrty. When Lebron was 16 he was already 6 foot 5 and starting Varsity for St. Marry's. High school. During if 4 year stint at St. Mary's High school lebron racked up 2,646 points (avraging 31.6), 523 assists (avraging 4.6), and 892 rebounds (avraging 9.6). Lebron's senior year he led St. Mary's to a 26-1 record and they won the Division II state championship. In Highschool Lebron also played football as a wide receiver wearing number 9. He was ranked as the best highschool football player in the state of Ohio his senior year. He had between 99-108 receptions and 1,912 yards and 23-28 touchdowns in 2 seasons. He had offers to play at some of the top football schools such as Ohio State, Alabama, and Notre Dame.
